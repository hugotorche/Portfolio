Key points:
  • All men are beings of reason and are equal before knowledge. The problem is the way they use this reason: the method.

  • The first step in reaching the truth is to put aside one's opinions and knowledge to reform one's mind. Independence of mind is the key: one must draw knowledge from oneself.

  • To reform your mind, you need a method: doubt (retain only the obvious), divide the difficulty (simplify a problem), lead by order (from the simplest to the most difficult), check that you have not forgotten anything.

  • To be able to think serenely, you need a provisional morality: prefer conformism/moderation, be firm in your opinions/actions, limit your will to your field of action, cultivate your reason.

  • The first certainties are: the cogito (I think therefore I am), the soul (thought) and God (perfect being).

  • Matter and the human body are purely mechanical. Only the soul distinguishes humans from animals (soul/matter dualism).

  • Technology is at the service of man, but man must not take himself for God and must respect the laws of nature.
