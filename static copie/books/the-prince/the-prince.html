Key points:
  • A prince can only rely on his own strength and must do everything to build an army.

  • He must know how to adapt his strategies according to the territory to be acquired and governed.

  • He owes his salvation more to the fear he inspires than to the affection of the people.

  • A skillful prince knows how to win the sympathy of the people and follow his instincts, even if it means offending the elites.

  • A citizen wishing to exercise power can achieve it through his virtue or through perfidy.

  • The best way to keep a conquest is to establish colonies or reside there.

  • A prince should never seek to escape from war at the risk of merely postponing it.
